Johann Rupert thinks it’s time to unbundle FirstRand

by John Nunoupdated on December 18, 2019December 18, 2019Leave a Comment on Johann Rupert thinks it’s time to unbundle FirstRand
Johann Rupert, president of Remgro, said Wednesday that it is the “end of the era” as his investment holding company and Rand Merchant Bank Holdings (RMH) are planning to unbundle their share of the country’s largest financial institution by market capitalization, FirstRand. He reflected on the 40 years that have passed since he founded Rand Merchant Bank in 1979. He said that despite considerable internal opposition within Remgro, he considered his decision to exchange shares in Anglo American mining companies for shares in the financial services company in the early 1990s to be one of the best in his career. “Once I went down one of the shafts and realized what a tough business it was, I knew it was not possible to simultaneously run a consumer goods company and a mining business,” he said. Rupert’s famous supporters were GT Ferreira, Laurie Dippenaar and Paul Harris, who founded FirstRand and built it into the current R378 billion financial services powerhouse.  “We simply can’t have 40% of our net asset value tied up in a business where we have no control or influence, nor that we have sought to influence. It just no longer made sense for us,” Rupert said. Jannie Durand, CEO of Remgro, accepted that FirstRand had outgrown the system. “FirstRand is now like a child that has grown up and left home. With the costs of the structure outweighing the benefits, the time has come to unbundle it…,”  Durand said. He said that the company will continue to find the most effective way to place FirstRand’s shares in the hands of Remgro’s shareholders and aims to table another way by the end of February next year.
